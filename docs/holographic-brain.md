# Holographic brain

The human face of brainpick is the movie scene: your knowledge floating as a
holographic brain — clusters gathered into lobes, edges firing as agents
write, spun and pinched with your fingers, on a desktop or a phone. It is
not decoration over fake data; the hologram renders exactly the graph agents
query, which is the point of the "one brain, two faces" principle.

Two layouts, one scene. Every node carries two positions — its place in the
flat 2D **cosmos** (the analytic mode: semantic zoom, search-as-flight, tag
and orphan lenses) and its place inside the 3D **brain**. The morph between
them is a GPU interpolation with per-node stagger: one gesture and the star
map streams into a brain, or back.

The brain form is computed, not painted: a force layout runs inside a signed
distance field of a brain mesh, so nodes fill the volume; community
detection over the [compile pipeline](compile-pipeline.md)'s link graph
assigns clusters to anatomical regions — your topic clusters literally
become lobes. The aesthetic is honest hologram: additive glow, a
fresnel-rimmed translucent shell, depth fog, selective bloom, slow idle
rotation.

It is alive by construction. [Live deltas](live-deltas.md) stream every
change: new concepts materialize beside their neighbors, deletions fade, and
recent edits travel as light pulses along edges — watching an agent think is
the default demo. A time scrubber replays the brain's growth from its
history, and the layer toggle swaps between explicit links and the
[knowledge graph tier](knowledge-graph-tier.md)'s entities.

And it ships as an installable, touch-first PWA from day one: one-finger
orbit, pinch dolly, two-finger spin, offline snapshot of your last brain,
and a mobile GPU budget (instanced sprites, focus-radius labels, node caps
with cluster aggregation) so a phone gets the wow without the fan noise.
